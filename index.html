About

I work at the intersection of applied AI, education systems, and policy evaluation. My research focuses on how algorithmic and platform-mediated systems operate within constrained institutional environments, and how those systems can be measured, evaluated, and improved using computational methods.

I build and apply Python- and SQL-based analysis pipelines to study large-scale education datasets, digital trace data, and survey evidence. My work combines computational social science, machine learning-assisted text analysis, and mixed-methods policy research to support system-level decision-making.

I am Co-founder and Chief Research Officer of the Classroom Tech Transparency Project, where I develop privacy-preserving tools and computational methods for analyzing how educational technologies are used in practice across schools and households.

I recently completed my PhD in Lifelong Learning (Minor in Comparative and International Education) at Penn State University. I have held research and policy roles at the World Bank, UNESCO IIEP, OECD, and the Global Partnership for Education, working on education systems analysis, teacher workforce policy, and digital learning infrastructure.

Earlier in my career, I taught mathematics and science in secondary and adult education settings, including work with refugee and immigrant learners, and served as a Peace Corps Volunteer focused on education and community learning systems.

My work is driven by a systems-level question: how do we design, evaluate, and govern digital learning environments so they are measurable, accountable, and aligned with human development goals?
